**Christine Capili** (she/her) is a Filipina American sociologist and Assistant of Professor of Sociology in the Humanities and Social Sciences at the University of La Verne. She earned her PhD in the Department of Sociology at the University of Oregon. Her research focuses on race & ethnicity, political sociology, demography, and quantitative methods. Capili's dissertation, "Creating Panethnicity: How Discrimination and Ethnonational Identity Affect Racialized Political Alignments," examines the salience of ethnonational and panethnic attachment and its relationship to discrimination, generational status, and political alignments in the United States.
